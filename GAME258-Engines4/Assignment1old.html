Making Mario 3-D and texture mapped (15/20)

Step 1: Finish building the MaterialComponent class. Use the code inside of Texture.cpp (from OpenGl class) to see how to load and store the texture (material).
You will need to make "textureID" a GLuint member variable of the MaterialComponent class. Probably should create a Get method for the ID value as well.

Step 2: Download the texture shaders from the main page and store them properly; while you're at it, get all of Mario's textures as well. In Scene0, change the default shaders to the texture shaders.

Step 3: Add a TextureComponent to Mario using one of his texture maps.

Step 4: In Render(), get a pointer to Mario's MaterialComponent.


Step 5: Bind Mario's texture and draw his mesh.




For added marks try the following: (Up to 5% more)

  • Extra: Mario was modelled with his back to us - Ooop's. Correct his orientation by his Tramsform Quaternion. 2%
  • Extra: In Scene0 Update(), spin Mario on his y-axis via the Transform Quaterion any way you can or wish. 3%
